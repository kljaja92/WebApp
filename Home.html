Serbian Climate

Spring

March: Daytime highs around 10°C, nights near 0°C. Weather is unstable, with frequent rain and occasional frost

April-May: Temperatures rise to 15-22°C, but sudden showers and thunderstorms are common.

Key feature: Spring is transitional and often unpredictable, making it less reliable for outdoor activities.

Summer

June: Warm, averaging 25°C, with longer sunny days.

July-August: Hot and dry, especially in lowland areas, with highs often exceeding 30°C. Belgrade and northern Serbia can experience heatwaves.

Highlands: Cooler, with pleasant temperatures around 20-25°C, making mountain resorts popular.

Key feature: Summers are hot and dry, ideal for festivals and river activities.

Autumn

September: Mild and pleasant, around 20°C, with less rainfall.

October: Cooler, 12-16°C, with colorful foliage and frequent fog in valleys.

November: Cold sets in, with temperatures dropping to 5-10°C and increased rainfall.

Key feature: Autumn is Serbia's most picturesque season, with vibrant landscapes and moderate weather.

Winter

December-January: Coldest months, with daytime highs around 3-6°C and frequent snow, especially in central and southern Serbia.

February: Still cold, but slightly milder, with temperatures rising toward 8°C.

Northern Serbia: Windy plains with dry, cool winds.

Mountains: Heavy snowfall, making them prime ski destinations.

Key feature: Winters are long, cold, and snowy, particularly in mountainous regions.
